On-grid rooftop solar for connected homes

Tie your solar plant directly to the grid, export surplus power and enjoy the benefits of government subsidy with a simple, low‑maintenance system.

How on-grid solar works

Panels on your roof generate DC power, the inverter converts it to AC that your home can use, and a bi‑directional meter tracks how much energy you consume and export.

  • • Daytime: solar powers your home first, reducing what you draw from the grid.
  • • Surplus: extra units automatically flow back to the grid and earn you credit via net metering.
  • • Night: you seamlessly draw energy from the grid, using banked credits where policy allows.

Subsidy overview

Central subsidy for on‑grid homes

Under the PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana, residential rooftop systems are eligible for tiered subsidy slabs (for example, ₹30k for 1 kW, ₹60k for 2 kW and up to ₹78k for 3 kW and above, as described in guides like SolarSquare’s subsidy article).

1–2 kW

Smaller homes

Subsidy can cover a significant portion of your upfront cost, helping you start small and expand later.

3 kW+

Maximum central support

Above 3 kW, the total central subsidy typically caps at ₹78k, with some states offering additional incentives.
